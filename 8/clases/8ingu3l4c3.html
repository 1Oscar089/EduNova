Spelling Rules for He, She, It (Affirmative)

In the simple present tense, when we talk about a third person (he, she, it), the verb changes:

  • Most verbs add -s (play → plays, read → reads).
  • Verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -s, -x, -o add -es (watch → watches, go → goes).
  • Verbs ending in consonant + y change to -ies (study → studies).

Negative Statements Structure

To make negative statements, we use an auxiliary verb. With he, she, it we use doesn't (does not). With I, you, we, they we use don't (do not).

Super important! The main verb after 'doesn't' remains in its base form (we do NOT add the 's').

Subject Auxiliary Base Verb Complement
I / You / We / They don't play chess.
He / She / It doesn't play (not plays!) chess.
